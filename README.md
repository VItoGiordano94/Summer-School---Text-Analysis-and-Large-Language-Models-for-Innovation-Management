# Text Analysis and Large Language Models for Innovation Management

This repository collects the teaching materials, practical exercises, datasets, code notebooks, examples, and additional resources developed for the **Summer School on Text Analysis and Large Language Models for Innovation Management** (https://www.unipi.it/didattica/corsi/summer-schools/text-analysis-large-language-models-for-innovation-management/), organised by the **University of Pisa**.

The Summer School provides an intensive learning experience at the intersection of **Text Analysis**, **Natural Language Processing (NLP)**, **Large Language Models (LLMs)**, and **innovation management**. It is designed for PhD students, researchers, and scholars interested in using computational text analysis to examine unstructured textual data, including patents, scientific publications, product reviews, business documents, and other sources relevant to innovation research and practice.

The repository supports both the conceptual and practical components of the Summer School. It introduces the main stages of a text-analysis research project, from defining the research objective and collecting textual data to preprocessing, representing, analysing, validating, and interpreting the results. Particular attention is dedicated to the relationship between the selected text-analysis method and the theoretical construct or research question that the method is intended to address.

A central objective of the Summer School is to encourage a **critical and methodologically rigorous use of NLP and LLMs**. The materials therefore go beyond the technical implementation of individual methods. They help participants understand when a method is appropriate, how it should be matched with a research objective, how its performance can be assessed, and how the resulting evidence can support credible knowledge claims.

The repository also contains resources for hands-on sessions and collaborative project work. Participants can use these materials to experiment with different text-analysis techniques, work with real-world datasets, compare alternative methodological approaches, and develop or improve their own research projects. The examples primarily focus on applications in innovation management, such as analysing patents, scientific publications, emerging technologies, market discourse, organisational communication, and other textual sources associated with innovation processes.

The repository is intended to serve not only as a source of materials during the Summer School but also as a reusable learning resource after the programme. Participants are encouraged to adapt the notebooks, workflows, and examples to their own research questions while carefully considering the theoretical assumptions, methodological limitations, and validation requirements associated with each technique.

## Main Learning Objectives

Through these materials, participants will be able to:

1. understand the principal concepts and terminology associated with text analysis, NLP, and LLMs;
2. identify appropriate textual sources for innovation and management research;
3. select text-analysis methods that are consistent with their research objectives;
4. implement foundational and advanced NLP techniques;
5. use Large Language Models critically and responsibly in research workflows;
6. assess the validity, reliability, reproducibility, and interpretability of text-based measures;
7. recognise the limitations, risks, and potential biases of computational text-analysis methods;
8. apply the acquired knowledge to an individual or collaborative research project.

## Intended Audience

The repository is primarily intended for participants in the Summer School, including PhD students, researchers, and scholars working in innovation management, strategic management, organisation studies, entrepreneurship, business, and related fields. However, the materials may also be useful to anyone interested in learning how text analysis and Large Language Models can be applied rigorously to management and social-science research.

## About the Summer School

The **Text Analysis and Large Language Models for Innovation Management Summer School** combines methodological lectures, interactive discussions, practical sessions, expert presentations, and collaborative project work. Its broader goal is to provide participants with a practical toolbox for analysing textual data while developing the critical awareness required to use NLP and LLM-based methods in a transparent, reliable, and theoretically meaningful way.
